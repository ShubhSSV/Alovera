Plant Information


Plant Name: Aloe Vera


Benefits: Skin Health: Aloe Vera is renowned for its soothing and healing properties. It is often used to treat burns, cuts, and skin irritations. It hydrates the skin and is commonly found in cosmetics, creams, and lotions. Digestive Aid: Consuming Aloe Vera juice can help soothe and promote healthy digestion. It may alleviate symptoms of acid reflux and improve gut health. Immune System Support: Aloe Vera contains antioxidants that help fight inflammation and boost the immune system. Wound Healing: It accelerates the healing of wounds and burns by promoting cell regeneration. Anti-Inflammatory Properties: Aloe Vera has anti-inflammatory effects, making it beneficial for conditions like arthritis. Oral Health: Aloe Vera juice can help improve dental health and reduce plaque buildup.



Season: Growing Season: Aloe Vera grows year-round in warm climates but thrives best in the spring and summer months when temperatures are warmer. Dormancy: In cooler climates, Aloe Vera may enter a dormant phase during fall and winter, requiring less water and care.



Region: Native Regions: Aloe Vera is native to the Arabian Peninsula but is now cultivated worldwide. Ideal Climates: It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with well-draining soil and plenty of sunlight. Common Areas: Aloe Vera is widely grown in Mexico, the Caribbean, and parts of the Mediterranean. It can also be found in many home gardens and nurseries.



Age: Lifespan: Under optimal conditions, Aloe Vera plants can live for 5 to 25 years. They can remain productive for several years if cared for properly. Maturity: Aloe Vera typically takes 3 to 4 years to reach full maturity, at which point it can be harvested for its gel and leaves.
